yes # Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **KCCEMSR students** seeking railway concession forms. They're on their phones between classes, booking a slot to visit the office instead of queuing physically.
- **Admin staff** at the registrar's office who process, verify, and sign concession forms during designated time slots.

## Product Purpose

Digitize the physical railway concession form pipeline at K.C. College of Engineering. Replace the queue-outside-the-office workflow with a live slot calendar, OTP-gated college-email sign-in, and pre-filled concession forms ready for the registrar to sign. Success looks like: no student waits in vain, no staff manages an unwieldy paper queue, every visit yields a signed form.

## Brand Personality

Clean, fast, trustworthy. Confident and direct — no corporate hedging, no excessive polish. Feels built by and for students, not "enterprise software." Uses conviction over decoration: every element earns its place. Communicates with clarity and candor.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS calendar aesthetics (Calendly clones, enterprise booking patterns)
- AI-generated design tells (gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics, repeating decoration, two-axis grid backgrounds, hand-drawn SVG illustrations)
- Corporate or "enterprise admin panel" visual language
- Anything that feels older, stiffer, or more formal than the college audience

## Design Principles

1. **Conviction over convention.** Don't reach for a pattern because "landing pages do this." Reach for it because it's the clearest path for the user.
2. **Speed is a feature.** The whole point is replacing a wait. The interface should feel fast, responsive, and never waste the user's time.
3. **Candid over polished.** Clear, direct communication. No marketing fluff, no hedging. Speak like a capable peer, not a corporation.
4. **Young without being juvenile.** Energetic, direct, confident. Not playful, cartoony, or gamified.
5. **Do one thing well.** Each screen has exactly one job. No feature creep, no crowded layouts, no split attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA as a baseline (4.5:1 text contrast, keyboard navigable interactive elements, visible focus indicators)
- Respects `prefers-reduced-motion` — parallax and scroll effects degrade gracefully to static
- Touch targets ≥ 40px for mobile users
- No special color deficiency requirements beyond default AA contrast
